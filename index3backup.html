About

The dynamics that foster disease emergence and transmission world-wide continue to expand in complexity and scope, thus creating unprecedented new challenges to solving local, regional, and global problems of animal and human health and to mitigating their social, economic, and political consequences. Global efforts toward sustaining animal and human health must have knowledge of disease incidence, as well as rapidly changing influences of competing factors in global health, including environmental changes, global warming, economic and political landscapes, and international movement and travel of humans and animals. Prerequisite to understanding changing local, regional, or global distributions of disease or disease risks is the rapid access to current information that can provide the necessary situational awareness of disease and of factors potentially associated with disease risk.

The Disease BioPortal is a web-based system that provides real-time or near-real time access to local, regional, and global disease information and other associated data (e.g. animal and human demographics, trade, climatic and environmental variables, etc). The system provides access to publicly available databases, as well as to private data through secure routing and sharing mechanisms. Tools are available for spatio- temporal display, graphics, phylogenetic and social network analysis of the data, as well as for downloading or uploading data. The Disease BioPortal is operated and maintained by the Center for Animal Disease Modeling and Surveillance at the University of California, Davis (http://www.vetmed.ucdavis.edu/cadms/) and is supported through a consortium of national and international institutions, agencies, and organizations. To become involved or for more information, please contact us (cadmsweb@ucdavis.edu)

What is useful for?
  • Disease BioPortal® provides a comprehensive, user-friendly, set of tools and capabilities to evaluate different types of disease, epidemiologic and phylogenetic data to better understand dynamics and risk factors for disease transmission → supports timely disease prevention and control and farm benchmarking.
  • Use to analyze, integrate and display data routinely collected by producers, practitioners, Ref. labs, etc. for risk assessment, outbreak investigation and emergency response.
    • Secure access and limited visualization/download of sensitive data
    • Ability to link to other sources of data and databases (OIE, GenBank, other reference laboratories, climatic and environmental data, etc.) without requirements of changing local databases and keeping confidentiality!
    • Contribution to the development of a near real-time surveillance system for infectious diseases
  • Supports the training, communication and technology transfer activities for producers, practitioners and other stakeholders → improving skills, awareness and risk perception.
